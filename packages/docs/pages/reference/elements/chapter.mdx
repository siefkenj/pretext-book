# `<chapter>`

The `<chapter>` tag is a major structural division of a
[`<book>`](./book.mdx). A chapter normally begins with a
[`<title>`](./title.mdx) and can contain
[`<introduction>`](./introduction.mdx), [`<section>`](./section.mdx),
and a closing [`<conclusion>`](./conclusion.mdx).

Use `<chapter>` when you want content organized and numbered at the
chapter level within a book.

## Examples

```ptx-example
<FRAGMENT parents="book">
    <chapter>
        <title>Functions</title>
        <section>
            <title>Function notation</title>
            <p>We write <m>f(x)</m> for the output of <m>f</m> at <m>x</m>.</p>
        </section>
    </chapter>
</FRAGMENT>
```

```ptx-example
<FRAGMENT parents="book">
    <chapter>
        <title>Sequences</title>
        <introduction>
            <p>We begin by looking at patterns in lists of numbers.</p>
        </introduction>
        <section>
            <title>Arithmetic sequences</title>
            <p>Each term differs from the previous one by a constant.</p>
        </section>
        <conclusion>
            <p>These ideas prepare us for series in the next chapter.</p>
        </conclusion>
    </chapter>
</FRAGMENT>
```
