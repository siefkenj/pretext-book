# `<claim>`

The `<claim>` tag creates a container for the statement of a claim
with optional `title`, `creator`, and `proof`. The structure of the `claim` tag
is completely analogous to that of the following tags:
[`algorithm`](./algorithm), [`corollary`](./corollary), [`fact`](./fact),
 [`identity`](./identity), [`lemma`](./lemma), [`proposition`](./proposition),
 and [`theorem`](./theorem).

## Examples
 ```ptx-example
<FRAGMENT parents="p">
<claim xml:id="claim-three-cubes">
    <creator>Booker and Sutherland</creator>
    <statement>
        <p>
        42 may be the answer to life, the universe and everything,
        but it is also the sum of three integer cubes.
        </p>
    </statement>
    <proof>
        <p>
        Not so easy.
        </p>
    </proof>
</claim>
</FRAGMENT>
```
