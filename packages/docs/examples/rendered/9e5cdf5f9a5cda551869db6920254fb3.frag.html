1.
Solve for \(x\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} 9x - 2= 4x + 5 \end{equation*}
Hint.
Add or subtract terms from both sides so that terms with an \(x\) are on the left side and all other terms are on the right.
Answer.
\(x = \frac{7}{5}\)
Solution.
First, combine the two terms that contain \(x\text{.}\) To do this, we subtract \(4x\) from both sides, obtaining.
\begin{equation*} 5x - 2 = 5\text{.} \end{equation*}
Next, we add \(2\) to both sides so only the term containing \(x\) remain on the left-hand side:
\begin{equation*} 5x = 7\text{.} \end{equation*}
Finally, divide both sides by \(5\) to get \(x\) by itself:
\begin{equation*} x = \frac{7}{5}\text{.} \end{equation*}
